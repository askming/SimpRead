# Ai Resistance Is Growing

**EDIT:** This post somehow made its way to the front page of a popular news hub. It generated some (mostly) good discussion there; the traffic received from the post was completely overwhelming, however. It also must have touched a nerve, as there have been quite a few obviously malicious attempts from a handful of IP addresses to overload the server with thousands of requests to this page. 😞 If I’d been on a cheap shared hosting service, my site would have almost certainly been brought offline. To stop the onslaught, I temporarily blocked all traffic to this URL while writing this quick response.

 
I left social media for a reason; the small web was (and still is) an escape from the anonymous mudslinging that tends to accompany viral posts. I am not an expert in AI whatsoever, and yet some commenters scrutinized what I said as though I _am_ an expert who should have known better. Someone even expressed that I shouldn’t be seen in a better light than “a group that goes around burning libraries.” This was especially saddening to read, as I _love_ libraries, and I also love sharing what I know with other people through my website.

 
With this post, I simply wanted to link to some anti-AI developments I learned about recently. I thought the small number of people who follow my blog would find it interesting. I know you can’t control how and where your work gets shared on the internet, but if I’d known it would generate so much negative attention on such a large platform, I probably wouldn’t have even posted it in the first place.

 
In future, I think I’ll just keep my thoughts on AI to myself. 😕 I’m ultimately here to have fun and enjoy the small web, so if my AI-related posts detract from that, then they’re simply not worth it.

 
My thanks to those of you who kept the discussion civil.
